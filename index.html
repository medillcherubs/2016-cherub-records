  • Who has been spit listed the most?

    AMANDA ROOKER
    was spit listed eight times

    When Rooker came to cherubs, she expected to be one of the students who never got spit listed. Be on time to class, stay on your floor after floor hours, don't jaywalk. It sounded easy to Rooker at first, she said. She was wrong.

    "One time I got spit listed for missing spit list," Rooker said. "It's become a routine for me, but not one that I like."

  • Who had the longest transcription notes?

    EMMA MITNICK
    wrote a 20377-word transcription

    Mitnick spoke with a sexual education trainer for 40 minutes. Only 17 of the 20377 words made it to her final draft.

    "This woman just went on and on," Mitnick said. "I tried cutting her off, but she wouldn't let me. So I let her talk, and it took me forever to transcribe."

  • Who had the most number of sources that didn't call back?

    SAMANTHA MEADE
    called 45 sources who didn't call back

    When Meade called doctors for her trend story on oral immunotherapy, she was often sent to the voice mailbox of her sources. She said every time she heard the voice message options such as "if you are having a medical emergency, please call 911" or “please pick up your prescription” she didn’t know what to do and felt "guilty" using those extension lines.

    "I didn't need 911 and I didn’t have an allergy attack," Meade said. "I was just a poor journalist who needed a quote."
